Recipe #1

  • Time: 10 min
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients Quantity
All-purpose flour 2 cups
teaspoon baking soda 1
teaspoon saltr 1
cup unsalted butter, room temperature 1
cup white sugar 3/4
cup brown sugar 3/4
large eggs 2
teaspoons vanilla extract 2
cups semisweet chocolate chips 2

Step 1: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

Step 2: In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda, and salt.

Step 3: In a large bowl, cream together butter, white sugar, and brown sugar until smooth. Beat in eggs, one at a time, then stir in vanilla.

Step 4: Gradually add the flour mixture to the butter mixture and mix until just combined. Fold in chocolate chips.

Step 5: Drop dough by rounded tablespoons onto the prepared baking sheet.

Step 6: Bake for 8 to 10 minutes, or until lightly golden. Remove from oven and let cool on the pan for 2 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

Recipe #2

  • Time: 7 min
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients Quantity
medium-sized potatoes 4
tbsp vegetable oil 2
teaspoon saltr 1
tsp salt (or to taste) 1
Optional seasonings (e.g. black pepper, paprika, garlic powder)

Step 1: Rinse and peel the potatoes, then thinly slice them using a mandoline or a sharp knife.

Step 2: Place the sliced potatoes in a bowl of cold water and let soak for at least 30 minutes. This will help remove some of the starch and improve their crispiness.

Step 3: Drain the potatoes and pat them dry with paper towels.

Step 4: In a large bowl, toss the potato slices with oil and salt (and any additional seasonings, if desired).

Step 5: Heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add the potato slices to the skillet in a single layer, making sure they are not overlapping.

Step 6: Cook the potato chips for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until they are golden and crispy. Remove them from the skillet with a slotted spoon and transfer to a paper towel-lined plate to drain.

Step 7: Repeat the cooking process with the remaining potato slices.

Step 8: Serve the potato chips immediately, while they're still hot and crispy.
